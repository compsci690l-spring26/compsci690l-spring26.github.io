Assignments

There will be four written/coding assignments, including Assignment 0. Assignment 0 will be released on February 6 and provides a reference for the overall difficulty of later assignments, as well as expectations for your preliminary knowledge. Assignment 0 will be automatically full marks if you complete it.

Subsequent assignments are due approximately three weeks after their release date. All assignments are submitted through Gradescope.

Late/Make-Up Work Policy

Deadlines for this course are strict. A submission made just one minute after the deadline will receive zero credit. You are strongly encouraged to submit your work well in advance of any deadline.

To provide some flexibility in completing assignments, you have a total of five free late days that you can use for submitting assignments only. The TA must be notified before the due date to use late days. You will be charged one late day for submitting an assignment within 24 hours after the due date, two late days for submissions made within 48 hours after the due date, and so on. After you have used up your late days, late assignments will not count for credit except in exceptional circumstances (e.g., illness documented by a doctor's note).

Note: Late days apply only to assignments, not to seminars or final project milestones.

Seminars

Students work in groups of 2–3 to present research papers. There are 3 sessions per week, with each presentation lasting 20 minutes followed by 5 minutes of Q&A. Presentation slides must be uploaded by 3:00 PM on the Monday before the presentation.

Final Research Project

The final project is the major component of this course, allowing students to explore any topic related to Generative AI. Projects can involve theoretical or practical research. The project is completed in groups of 3–4 students (groups must be formed before the proposal deadline) and has the following milestones:

  • Proposal (5%): Due March 13
  • Milestone (5%): Due April 10
  • Final Video & Write-up (30%): Due May 11

Attendance Policy

Students enrolled in this course are encouraged to attend and actively participate in every class meeting. However, you have the option to skip "regular" class meetings (i.e., those without assignments or exams) if necessary. We understand that each student can independently decide how to allocate their time effectively.

Please note that you are responsible for any material covered during classes you miss. If you are unable to attend, it is your duty to obtain missed notes from a classmate and collect any handouts distributed by the instructor. The instructor will not provide class notes or a comprehensive summary of the missed class via email or a brief recap.

Academic Honesty

All work submitted in this course must be the student's own. Any instance of academic dishonesty, including plagiarism or unauthorized collaboration, will be dealt with according to the university's academic honesty policy.
